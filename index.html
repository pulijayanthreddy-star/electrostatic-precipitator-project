Electrostatic Precipitator

Introduction

An Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) is a pollution control device used to remove dust and smoke particles from industrial gases using electrostatic forces.

Working Principle

1. Dust-laden gas enters the chamber.

2. High voltage is applied to the ionizing wire.

3. Corona discharge charges the dust particles.

4. Electric field pulls particles toward collection plates.

5. Dust sticks to plates and clean air exits.

Main Components

  • Ionizing wire
  • Collection plates
  • High voltage DC power supply
  • Gas flow chamber
