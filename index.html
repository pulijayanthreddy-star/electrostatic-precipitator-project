This project demonstrates the working principle of an Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP). It includes theory, explanation and a simple simulation.

Introduction

Electrostatic precipitators are devices used to remove dust and smoke particles from industrial gases. They use electrostatic forces to collect particles on metal plates.

Working Principle

High voltage electrodes ionize the air and charge dust particles. The charged particles move toward oppositely charged plates and stick to them, removing them from the air stream.

Main Components

  • High voltage power supply
  • Discharge electrodes
  • Collection plates
  • Gas chamber
  • Dust removal system

Design Methodology

The simulation models particle motion inside an electrostatic field. Particles move across the chamber and are attracted toward collection plates.
